## 1. What Is Vegetable PU?

Polyurethane (PU) is a polymer — a molecular "network" forming a resistant, flexible and durable plastic.

**Petrochemical PU** = petroleum-derived. Emits toxic solvents, high carbon footprint.
**Vegetable PU (VPU)** = derived from **castor oil**. 100% solids, solvent-free, biodegradable under suitable conditions.

The difference is the raw material: castor is a plant grown in the Brazilian semi-arid region, renewable, and does not compete with food crops.

## 3. ⚠️ FUNDAMENTAL HANDLING RULE

> **Never dilute Vegetable PU.** The product is 100% solids, solvent-free. The two-component (A + B) formulation is chemically balanced by the manufacturer. Adding any thinner breaks the polymerization reaction and compromises adhesion, strength and durability.

**What to do with leftovers?**
- The product cures by chemical reaction — it doesn't "dry" by evaporation
- Mix only the amount you'll use within the pot life (~20 min)
- Once cured, it cannot be dissolved or recovered

**Tool and surface cleaning:**
- ✅ Use **cereal alcohol** (ethanol) for cleaning tools, brushes and surfaces before curing
- ✅ Use **acetone** or **isopropyl alcohol** for removing fresh (uncured) resin
- ❌ Do not use aggressive solvents (kerosene, thinner) — they may react chemically
- ❌ Cured product can only be removed mechanically (sanding)

## 5. Explaining the Numbers (Didactic)

### 2 MPa tensile strength — what does it mean?
One **megapascal (MPa)** equals ~10 kg/cm² pressure. So 2 MPa = **20 kg per square centimeter**.
> *Imagine an adhesive that holds 20 kg hanging from every square centimeter of bonded area.*

### 0.01% water absorption (MAMONEX) — what does it mean?
A 1 kg foam absorbs **0.1 gram of water** after 48h submerged.
> *A glass of water spilled on PU foam — it barely gets wet.*

### 0.0452 W/mK thermal conductivity — what does it mean?
Still air is ~0.024 W/mK. PU foam (0.045) insulates **almost as well as still air**.
> *A 10 cm PU foam wall insulates as well as a 1-meter thick brick wall.*

### Elongation >50% — what does it mean?
A 10 cm PU membrane can stretch to **over 15 cm** without tearing.
> *Accommodates thermal expansion, structural movement, fine cracks — without rupturing.*

## 8. Application Guidelines (General Standard)

1. **Surface:** must be **dry, clean and free of contaminants**
2. **Do not apply on wet surfaces or rainy days**
3. **Mixing:** prepare only the volume needed within the pot life (~20 min)
4. **Ratios:** follow **exactly** the A:B ratio indicated on each product's datasheet
5. **Stirring:** component B must be stirred before use to disperse additives
6. **Coats:** apply thin, uniform layers
7. **Recoat interval:** ~2 hours (max. 6h — after 6h interlayer adhesion is lost)
8. **Average coverage:** 0.7 to 1.0 kg/m² (3 to 5 coats, depending on requirements)
9. **Storage:** original sealed packaging, avoid temperature fluctuations
10. **Shelf life:** 6 months from manufacturing date
